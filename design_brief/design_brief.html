E-Waste

Design Brief

What is e-waste?

Electronic Waste, otherwise known as e-waste, is a term used to describe any electronic product that has reached the end of its usability.

E-waste is prolific

An estimated 50 million tons of e-waste is produced each year. This is because toxic chemicals, such as mercury and sulphur, can be found in everyday products like fluorescent tubes and lead-acid batteries

What's the Big Deal?

Improperly disposing of e-waste can lead to massive environmental damage. E-waste can produce harmful, possibly fatal, agents, such as airborne dioxides and carcinogens.

How can I help?

You can reduce e-waste by dismantling the products that produce it for both functioning and non-functioning parts. You can then re-use or recycle the salvageable parts and safely destroy the broken or unuseable parts.

How will that help?

E-Waste is a very interesting factor within the larger problem of environmental pollution. E-waste only actually accounts for about 2% of waste by itself, but produces about 70% of all environmental pollution. This is because only 12% of all e-waste is actually waste.

What's the other 88%?

The other 88% of so-called e-waste can either be re-used in other products or scrapped for useful parts. In most cases, e-waste also has precious metals, which have a cash value.

The only way forward, if we are going to improve the quality of the environment, is to get everybody involved.
