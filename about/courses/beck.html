Mr. Joshua Beck

ETA Teacher - Information Technologies

Mr. Beck is the teacher of information technologies. He believes in a practice of continual learning. He taught himself to use Linux and subsequently became a power user. He continues to do so as he passes on his knowledge, both in the classroom and extracurricular activities, such as CyberPatriot.

Principles of Information Technology (0.5 Credit)

This is an introductory course to Information Technology. Students in this class will discuss the basics of programming, as well as networking computer networking and security.

Communication Applications (0.5 Credit)

Students in this class are able to effectively construct and bolster their public speaking skills while simultaneously earning their necessary speech credit.

Computers and Inter-networking Technologies I (1 Credit)

In this course, students progress through rigorous modules in order to prepare for their TestOut PCPro certification, a CompTIA A+ equivalent. This opportunity allows students to pursue future jobs.

Computers and Inter-networking Technologies II (1 credit)

This class allows students to expand their knowledge and pursue further CompTIA certifications, such as the Network+ and Security+. These exams test the student beyond the entry-level concepts of the A+.

Computer Programming (1 Credit)

Mr. Beck's Computer Programming teaches fundamental concepts of navigating the Linux operating system. Students will also learn Java and become basically proficient in writing source code.

Advanced Computer Programming (1 Credit)

The advanced version of Computer Programming expands a student's Java knowledge and explores developing Android applications. This includes potential to publish to the Google Play Store.

CyberPatriot (Extracurricular Activity)

Students that are members of this program learn about computer and network security in order to form teams and compete worldwide against thousands of other teams in a scored competition. Visit http://www.uscyberpatriot.org/ to learn more.
