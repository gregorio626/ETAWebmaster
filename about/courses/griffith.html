ETA STAFF


Mr. Brian Griffith

ETA Teacher - Engineering/Robotics/Electronics

Mr. Griffith not only completed college, he also served in the army. In his years studying environmental sciences, geology, and anthropology, he developed a desire to pass on his knowledge through teaching. He finds working at ETA to be both challenging and rewarding.

Cybernetics and Automation (1 Credit)

Students will use a variety of computer hardware and software to complete their work. Students also explore career opportunities, employer expectations, and educational needs in the industry.

Engineering Mathematics (1 Credit)

Students use a variety of mathematical processes to solve problems involving data acquisition, spatial applications, electrical measurement, manufacturing processes, pneumatics, hydraulics, and more.

Geographic Information Systems (1 Credit)

In this class, students learn about basic GIS concepts including spatial data structures, projections and coordinate systems, georeferencing, supporting software, global positioning systems, and more.
